🌿 Flower Assembly Guide — Oct 7 & 8
📦 Wednesday, Oct 7 — Airport Pickup & Conditioning
🚗 Bring: all buckets (16 regular + 7 short), water jugs, a tarp or trash bags for the car. Jackie's number: 888.462.9374
  • 1
    Drive to Redmond Municipal Airport (RDM)
    Pick up Delta Airlines shipment — this is the main flower order. Confirm tracking with Jackie if anything is unclear.
  • 2
    Check FedEx for garland delivery (separate)
    3×8' eucalyptus garlands ship separately via FedEx — should arrive same day or day before. Track via FedEx number once Lauren sends it.
  • 3
    Get home and fill all buckets with cool water immediately
    Use the bucket list below — one type of flower per bucket so you can find things easily tomorrow.
  • 4
    Unbox everything right away — flowers need water ASAP
    Don't leave them boxed up. Get them in water within 30 minutes of getting home.
  • 5
    Cut every stem at a 45° angle before placing in water
    Cut about 1 inch off. Angle = more surface area = more water uptake. Do this under water if you can.
  • 6
    Strip leaves that would sit below the waterline
    Leaves in water = bacteria = dead flowers faster. Keep the pretty stuff above water only.
  • 7
    Store overnight in a cool, dark space — NOT the fridge
    A garage, laundry room, or cool basement is perfect. Avoid direct sunlight or heat.
✂️ Thursday, Oct 8 — Assembly Day (Your PTO Day)
☕ Set up before you start: large cleared table or floor with tarp · clippers · floral tape · bind wire · ribbon · boutonniere pins · water tubes · zip ties · wet paper towels for wrapping finished pieces
  • 1
    Make the 4 Boutonnieres first
    Small, easy, good warm-up. Wrap with boutonniere tape → cover with ribbon. Lauren is providing the magnets.
  • 2
    Make the Dog Collar
    Lauren will send the pre-made frame. Use clippings/scraps from the boutonnieres. Affix blooms to frame per her tutorial.
  • 3
    Make the 2 Bridesmaid Bouquets
    Make both at the same time so they match. Wrap stems with bind wire → cover with ribbon.
  • 4
    Make the MOH Bouquet
    Slightly larger than bridesmaid bouquets. Pulls from the same bunches as the Bride — these are shared bunches, not separate ones.
  • 5
    Make YOUR Bridal Bouquet — save the best for last 👰
    This is the hero piece. Take your time. Wrap with bind wire, cover bind wire with ribbon. When done, put it in water in a tall vase immediately.
  • 6
    Make the 2 Arch/Altar Sprays
    Two cages for one side, one cage for the opposite offset side. Zip ties go on at the venue. Use remaining clippings to fill out the sprays.
  • 7
    Make the 66 Bud Vases (put on a show, this takes a while)
    36 for round guest tables (4 per table × 9 tables) + 30 for head table (10 per 8' section × 3). Keep each vase simple — 2-3 stems each. Pack into Taylor's bankers boxes by table.
  • 8
    Wrap & store everything for transport
    Bouquets + boutonnieres: wet paper towel around stems, in water for the car. Bud vases: pack in bankers boxes. Garlands: keep in box until head table setup.
